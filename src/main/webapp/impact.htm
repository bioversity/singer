SINGER’s Impact

SINGER underpins the efforts of the Future Harvest Centres and others to alleviate poverty by making it easier to obtain and make use of genetic resources and related information.

SINGER’s impact on the lives of resource - poor farmers is essentially indirect and therefore difficult to assess. However, we take it as axiomatic that genetic diversity can be managed and used to improve the food security and livelihoods of the poorest people. SINGER makes a vital contribution to that effort.

Aside from its direct involvement with numerous breeding programmes, SINGER has also had other important functions:

  • The restoration of local genetic resources in times of crisis
  • Tracking movement of resources to examine possible violations of in-trust agreements
  • Pre-screening accessions for particular traits
  • Locating under-represented areas for future collecting missions
  • Helping to identify accessions for repatriation
  • Supporting research into issues such as taxonomic relationships among accessions.
