Networks of knowledge

SINGER is now a driving force in information networking inside and outside the CGIAR. It meets the needs of researchers, plant breeders, farmers and conservers in their efforts to improve production and sustain food security. It changed from being a source of information to a dynamic network that harnesses expertise and information to further the conservation and use of plant genetic resources.

The SINGER model and the expertise, tools and infrastructure of the network are available to assist others to establish their own information networks. For example, SINGER has been contracted to implement the European catalogue of crop genetic resources collections (EURISCO). It was asked to help develop an Internet ­based information system for the Asian Vegetable Research and Development Center (AVRDC).

SINGER encourages networking on the basis of crop and geographical regions. Currently, to link holders of wheat, barley and sweet potato collections worldwide, pilot networks are being established. These efforts are being led by the Future Harvest Centres responsible for those crops.

  • Wheat- The International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center (CIMMYT),
  • Barley- The International Center for Agricultural Research in the Dry Areas (ICARDA)
  • Sweet potato - The International Potato Center (CIP)

With efforts such as these, SINGER aims to be a major contributor to the development of the global information system for plant genetic resources envisaged by the FAO.
